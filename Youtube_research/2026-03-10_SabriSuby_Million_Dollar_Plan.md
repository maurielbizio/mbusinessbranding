## Step 1 — Finding the Right Business

### 4 Market Criteria
1. **$10M potential** — Don't enter thinking small
2. **Growth** — Market must be expanding, not shrinking
3. **Timing** — Already growing but about to explode
4. **Competition** — Money already changing hands (proven demand)

### Business Model Requirements
- **80%+ gross margins** — Enough to invest in ads and scale
- **Recurring revenue** — "Be in the reorder business, not the order business"
- **Low capital to start** — No heavy upfront investment
- **Scalable without more of your time** — Otherwise it's a job, not a business

### Target Customer Profile
- Has a **"bleeding neck"** — immediate, pressing, painful problem
- The greater the pain → the higher the price you can charge

### The Business: AI Implementation
- Market: $190B today → $4T by 2033
- Target: Cash-rich businesses (NOT startups)
- Sell **outcomes**, not technology

## Step 2 — The Godfather Offer

### Core Principle
> "A transaction takes place where value exceeds price. It's not about being cheaper — it's about giving so much value that the price feels irrelevant."

### Weak vs. Strong Offer Example
| Type | Example |
|------|---------|
| Weak (vanilla) | "I help businesses implement AI. I charge $3,000/month." |
| Godfather | "I'll build you an AI system that saves you 20 hours/week. If it doesn't work, it's free. Takes 7 days to set up." |

### Godfather Offer Formula
1. **Specific outcome** — not a service, a result with a number
2. **Risk reversal** — you absorb the risk, not the client
3. **Speed** — fast setup / quick wins
4. **No-brainer framing** — "stupid to say no"

### How to Build It
- Start with the most outrageous offer imaginable
- Then dial it back to what you can actually deliver
- Do NOT start with what's deliverable and try to make it sound good

### Why It Works
- A compelling offer is 10x more powerful than a convincing salesperson
- Prospects show up to calls ready to buy
- Removes friction — more yeses, more profitable business

## Step 3 — Getting Clients (Cold Outreach)

### Principle: Use Human Capital, Not Financial Capital

Two weapons:

### Weapon 1: The Shotgun (Cold Email)
- **Volume:** 400 emails/day
- **Tools:** Apollo, LinkedIn Sales Navigator (list building) + Woodpecker or Instantly (sending)
- **Key:** Personalized, humorous, human-looking emails
- **Goal:** Open a conversation — NOT close a sale
- **Format:** Short, quirky, animated GIFs, humor
- *"If you can get them to laugh, you can get them to buy."*

### Weapon 2: The Sniper Rifle (Pipe Bombs)
- **What it is:** Personalized Loom video sent to each prospect
- **Volume:** 10 per day
- **Watch rate:** 80-90%
- **Structure:** 80% value, 20% pitch/CTA
- **Identity trigger:** Show their website, LinkedIn profile — make it unmistakably personal
- **Notification:** Loom tells you when they've watched it → follow up immediately

### Pipe Bomb Script Framework
1. Reference something specific about their business (e.g., job posting for a bookkeeper)
2. Show them the solution in action on screen
3. Prove you can help them by actually helping them — no curtain
4. Simple CTA: "Reply with a thumbs up and I'll send it over"

## Key Takeaways for Mbusiness Branding

1. **The "bleeding neck" framing** applies directly — home service businesses are bleeding when their phone goes unanswered, no reviews come in, or their website doesn't convert. Lead with that pain.

2. **Godfather Offer** — Apply this to the Founders VIP Program. Don't say "AI website + AI receptionist + reputation management." Say: "We'll get you 5 new customer calls booked in 30 days or you don't pay."

3. **Pipe Bomb strategy** — Highly applicable for outreach to local home service businesses. Record a Loom showing their Google profile with no reviews, broken website, or missed leads, then show the fix.

4. **Cold email volume** — 400/day is the threshold. Use Apollo to build lists of local plumbers, HVAC, roofers, etc. by zip code.

5. **Outcomes over features** — Never sell "AI implementation." Sell "5 more booked jobs per week while you sleep."
